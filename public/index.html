Last updated: April 2026

1. What we collect

We collect your email address when you sign up. We store the flashcard decks you generate so you can access them later. We do not collect payment information directly; that is handled by Stripe.

2. How we use your data

Your email is used to authenticate your account and send transactional emails (such as email confirmation). Your deck data is used solely to provide the Service to you.

3. AI processing

Your notes are sent to an AI API to generate flashcards. We do not store your raw notes. Only the generated flashcards are saved.

We do not sell your data. We do not share your data with third parties except as necessary to provide the Service (Supabase for database hosting, Stripe for payments).

About
Built by students,
for students.

Cardly started as a personal project. We were tired of spending hours turning lecture notes into flashcards by hand, so we built a tool that does it in seconds.

We're a small team of engineering students who wanted a smarter way to study. Cardly handles the tedious part: turning dense notes into focused, testable cards. So you can spend your time actually learning.

From PDF uploads to Anki exports, difficulty tracking to shareable decks, we keep building the features that make a real difference the night before an exam.
